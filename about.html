Background

How PixelCart Started

PixelCart was born out of frustration. Our founder, Brian Mwangi, spent weeks trying to buy a quality laptop in Nairobi — only to be met with overpriced grey-market products, no warranties, and payment methods that didn't work locally. He knew there had to be a better way.

In 2021, PixelCart launched as a small operation out of Westlands with 15 laptop models and a simple website. Within six months, word spread across universities and tech communities in Nairobi. By 2023 we had served over 1,000 customers — all paid for through M-Pesa, all backed by genuine manufacturer warranties.

Today PixelCart stocks over 120 laptop models across all major brands, offers repair and upgrade services, and remains committed to one thing — being the most trusted laptop store in Kenya.

Mission & Vision

The principles that guide every decision we make

Our Mission

To make premium, genuine laptops accessible to every Kenyan — through transparent pricing, local payment methods, and service that treats customers with respect.

  • Stock only genuine, warranty-backed laptops
  • Accept M-Pesa as the primary payment method
  • Deliver within Nairobi same day, anywhere in Kenya next day
  • Offer honest, no-hidden-fee pricing in KES
🔭

Our Vision

To become Kenya's most trusted technology retail platform — where every student, professional, and creator can confidently buy, upgrade, and maintain their laptops without compromise.

  • Expand to all major Kenyan cities by 2026
  • Build the largest laptop repair network in East Africa
  • Enable 10,000+ Kenyan tech users annually
  • Be the go-to recommendation for every campus community
